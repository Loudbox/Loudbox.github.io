Me

I started actively playing video games at around the age of 7, when I had a gameboy advance SP and a PS3. Pokemon Red, LittleBigPlanet, and Minecraft were the first games that I played which peaked my interest for multiple years. LittleBigPlanet and Minecraft especially held my attention for their allowed use of creativity and design of their own worlds and levels. This was the first time that I started designing my own parts of games. At around the age of 12 I became fluent in python after picking it up from an IT class in school, and started making python scripts for macros, algorithms, and other sorts of tools that I could use on a regular basis. At around the age of 14 I was competitively skilled in most games that I played, and I started researching how people created the games themselves. This is when I started making my own games involving actual programming and modelling, using Roblox Engine. I taught myself how to make 3D models and how to program in LUA, until I had created about 12 or so games in total. At the age of 16 I started expanding my programming vocabulary with other languages that appealed to me for game design, such as html, css, c#, go, and harlowe. After becoming fluent in harlowe, I created my first browser-based standalone game. Shortly after, I started learning how to code in c#, as well as how to use Unity and Blender for design, programming and modelling. After a few months of work, I had created my first prototype of a Unity standalone game. Even though it was unfinished, I was very proud of my 3D platformer game that I had created. I showed it to some of my friends, and they all had a blast playing it. This motivated me enough to start making 3 more Unity projects, enhancing my skills with programming using different Unity tools, such as visual scripting, character controllers, input managers and other more advanced scripts. Today, I am a college student taking a game design course, and I am planning on moving on to a more general Computer Science course for university.
